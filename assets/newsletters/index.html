2020 in Retrospect: The Year of the Pandemic

BRAC James P Grant School of Public Health, BRAC University looks back at 2020, a year ravaged by the COVID-19 pandemic, by recapping how the institution adapted to a nationwide lockdown; what activities and measures it undertook, and how it continued its work on critical health challenges affecting disadvantaged communities.

Education

As the world went into lockdown due to COVID-19, BRAC JPGSPH, BRACU shifted all educational activities of the Masters of Public Health (MPH) programme and Developing Midwives Project (DMP) online.

The Master of Public Health (MPH) Programme at BRAC James P Grant School of Public Health, BRAC University is a one-year full-time programme that offers transformative public health learning to develop next-generation public health leaders for 21st century public health challenges.

Training

In 2020, given the on-going crisis, the Centre for Professional Skills Development on Public Health (CPSD) conducted training, seminars, workshops, webinars, the majority of which were focused on COVID-19 and the challenges that came with it.
Read More →

Research

BRAC JPGSPH, BRACU conducted over 110 research projects in 2020 including 48 research that targeted issues, such as increased gender-based violence, stigma, maternal and child health, nutrition, mental health, etc which emerged as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic.
